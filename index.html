Climate Change

Keith Lomis

What is it?

Definition

A significant and long-lasting change in the Earth's climate and weather patterns, also called the climate crisis: serious problems that are being caused or likely to be caused by changes in the planets's weather, in particular Earth getting warmer as a result of human activity increasing the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere.

What causes it?

Green house gases

Any of the various gaseous compounds (such as carbon dioxide or methane) that absorb infrared radiation, trap heat in the atmosphere, and contribute to the greenhouse effect, defined as warming of the surface and lower atmosphere of a planet (such as Earth or Venus) that is caused by conversion of solar radiation into heat in a process involving selective transmission of short wave solar radiation by the atmosphere, its absorption by the planet's surface, and reradiation as infrared which is absorbed and partly reradiated back to the surface by atmospheric gases.

Burning coal and other fossil fuels help produce these gases. The ash from coal plants accumulates and releases carbon dioxide in large amounts. This map shows ash content from coal plants in each state.

What effects can this have?

Effects


The rise in green house gases is causing global temperature fluxuations that melt sea ice, increase sea levels, and put countless people in danger of floods.

Oh No! What can I do?

There are many things we can do!

Primarily, preventing the burning of fossil fuels and reducing the amount of energy we consume can helpful. To start, you can look into reducing your carbon footprint. Driving less, and buying a fuel efficient vehicles, are often mentioned but one should also consider consuming less energy in general and avoiding highly manufactured products or those that are shipped internationally. "Reduce, reuse, recycle" is a good motto when it comes to consumer goods that would otherwise end up in a landfill.
